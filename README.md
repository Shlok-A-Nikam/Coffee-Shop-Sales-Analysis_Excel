### 1. Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive sales analysis of a Coffee Shop retail dataset.
The objective is to explore transaction-level sales data and derive meaningful business insights that can help improve customer experience, optimize product offerings, and increase overall revenue.

#### The analysis focuses on:

-	Customer purchasing behavior
-	Product performance and category contribution
-	Store-wise and time-wise sales trends
-	Revenue generation and operational efficiency

### 2. Dataset Description
The dataset contains transactional sales records with the following important fields:

#### Store Information
- Store Location
#### Product Details
- Product ID
-	Product Category
-	Product Type
-	Product Detail
-	Size
- Unit Price
#### Transaction Details
-	Transaction Quantity
-	Total Bill
-	Day Name
-	Month Name
-	Hour
-	Day of Week
These fields enable multi-dimensional sales analysis across time, location, and product hierarchy.
### 3. Business Objectives
The key objectives of this analysis are:
- Measure total sales revenue and customer footfall
- Identify peak hours and peak days for sales
- Determine best-selling products and categories
- Compare performance across store locations
- Calculate average bill per person and order frequency
- Support data-driven decision-making for marketing and operations
### 4. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Based on the dashboard:
-	Total Sales: $6,98,812.33
-	Total Footfall: 149,116
-	Average Bill per Person: 4.69
-	Average Orders per Person: 1.44
These KPIs summarize the overall business performance and customer spending behavior.
### 5. Sales Trend Analysis
#### Day-Wise Sales
-	Sales are highest on weekdays, especially Monday to Friday.
-	Saturday shows comparatively lower revenue, indicating reduced customer activity.
#### Hour-Wise Quantity Ordered
-	Peak ordering occurs between 8 AM and 10 AM, matching morning coffee demand.
-	Orders gradually decline in the evening, with the lowest around 8 PM.
This indicates strong morning business dependency.
### 6. Product & Category Insights
#### Category Distribution
-	Coffee contributes the largest share of revenue (~39%).
-	Bakery and drinking chocolate also contribute significantly.
•	Other categories have smaller but meaningful participation.
Size-Wise Orders
-	Regular and Large sizes dominate customer preference.
-	Small size demand is minimal.
#### Top 5 Products by Sales
-	Barista Espresso
-	Brewed Chai Tea
-	Gourmet Brewed Coffee
-	Hot Chocolate
-	Brewed Black Tea
These products generate the highest revenue contribution.
### 7. Store Location Performance
Sales comparison across locations shows:
-	Certain stores (e.g., Hell’s Kitchen / Astoria / Lower Manhattan) contribute higher transaction volumes.
-	Location-wise analysis helps in:
  -	Inventory planning
  - Staff allocation
  - Promotional targeting

### 9. Key Insights
-	Morning hours drive maximum revenue.
-	Coffee category dominates overall sales share.
-	Regular size is the most preferred by customers.
-	A few top products generate major revenue contribution.
-	Sales vary significantly across store locations and weekdays
These insights can guide pricing, promotions, and staffing strategy.
